It seems like I read Sanderson's works in the wrong order. So much of the ideas within this book seep into the Stormlight Archive, so much so it's clear certain characters were frameworks for later characters. I don't see that as a detriment, but it was difficult not to compare when the similarities are so glaring.

The story was good for the most part. While Kelsier takes on a lot of the bulk of the interesting and driving parts of the narrative, eventually the other main character, Vin, does grow into her own. Again, extremely difficult not to compare them directly to Kaladin and Shallan, when so much of their development and even story actions are similar if not the exact same. Honestly the more I write about it the more it bothers me, but during reading it didn't too much so I'll refrain from lowering the score more than I currently have it.

Even themes are shared, with the conflict of Skaa and Parshendi being nearly identical. I do like the core concept of Hero didn't save the world, but I was also explicitly told that was what the story was about before reading. This isn't stated until nearly the end of the novel, meaning if I didn't already know that I would have been waiting the entire time for something more interesting to start driving the plot forward.

Allomancy is an interesting magic system, and leaves a lot of exploring once we know the rules established are anything but set in stone (or cast in metal). While the final fight was entertaining and fun, some of the other uses of Allomancy didn't come through as clearly as some of the Surges are later in Stormlight. Again, I find myself comparing this to Stormlight when it clearly stands on it's own two feet. I just really do prefer the larger expanding development of Roshar compared to Mistborn's setting. Given that I can't remember what their planet/continent is called is a good sign it wasn't as memorable. The maps in the beginning felt mostly useless, since the story took place in pretty much two locations of maybe 20 named places. There was some development of how their northern ecosystems look, and some of the people, but so little for such a large world.

It's truly a swing and a base hit. Maybe 1st or 2nd base. If good reads could do half scores, I'd give this a 3.5/5. Totally in the middle, but a little closer to a 3 than a 5. I'm looking forward to seeing how book 2 and 3 expand on Allomancy, and hopefully explore more of their world.
