This one really stunk. It's unfortunate that a lot of bookstagram loves this and recommends it every time someone is discussing disturbing fiction. Sold as a cross between Lord of the Flies and Battle Royale, I couldn't help but feel sorry for it by the end, since it will never live up to either's acclaim. I could dissect why we love disturbing fiction in the first place, but that's for another day. I could even get into why people liked this book, but honestly that's an even greater mystery than anything this work has to offer. 

It started out really strong; multiple narratives from unreliable narrators? Totally into that. What I wasn't into was how lazy it felt. Almost like the author knew how good of an idea it was, but forgot about having to actually execute and come up with interesting things for our narrators to discuss. The real killer of this is how boring our narrators are, and how we never hear from them again. It would have really helped the narrative had we heard from some characters at multiple points, maybe before and after certain interviews were conducted. Instead, it's all in an attempt to generate a timeline of events that's boring and unsubstantial. None of the characters felt real. None of their motives were genuine, save maybe the guy who went to hide in the hotel (incredibly smart, maybe the only interesting chapter/character perspective). 

Completely forgettable. The worst thing about this is someone on GoodReads compared this to The Warriors, which makes The Warriors look really bad.
